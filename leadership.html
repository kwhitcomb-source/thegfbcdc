Our Leadership Team

Committed to the Cause. Our leadership team recognizes the authority of God, and realizes that we are shepherds who serve under the Chief Shepherd, Jesus Christ. We desire to devote ourselves to prayer and to the ministry of the Word. Our passion and goal is to equip the believers for the work of the ministry until we all attain maturity in the fullness of Christ.

Church Leadership

The Greater First Baptist Church

The Greater First Baptist Church is guided by faithful leadership committed to spiritual growth, sound biblical teaching, and service. Leadership works collaboratively to support worship, discipleship, and meaningful engagement within the church and the surrounding community.

Rooted in a strong foundation and shared vision, the church continues to move forward with purpose, honoring its mission while responding to the needs of its congregation and community.

Reverend Robert Hood

Associate Minister

Rev. Robert Hood is an Associate Minister at The Greater First Baptist Church. Born and raised in Lynchburg, Virginia, he was nurtured at the historic New Vine Baptist Church and saved at a young age.

Education: Bachelor's Degree in Recreational Leadership, University of the District of Columbia (1982) Master's Degree in Biblical Studies, Maple Springs Baptist Bible College and Seminary (2010)

Ministry: Rev. Hood is committed to preaching, teaching, evangelizing, and educating both the saved and those seeking salvation. He was licensed to preach in August 2001, ordained in 2005, and has served in various ministerial roles including the Lay Ministry, Sunday School, Usher Board, Community Outreach, and as an armor bearer.

Professional Service: Currently serves as Director of Therapeutic Recreation and Acting Chaplain at The Stoddard Baptist Nursing Home. Previously directed Sports and Recreation for the District of Columbia Special Olympics, Inc., demonstrating God's calling to serve those in need and the less fortunate.

Community Leadership: Served on the Board of Directors for the 'Take Charge Program,' specializing in family strengthening, juvenile prevention, and intervention services for at-risk youth.
